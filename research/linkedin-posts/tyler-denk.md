My newsletter has 133k subs, makes a half million $$ a year, and takes me 5 hours a week.

The #1 question I get, by far, is “how do I grow my newsletter?”

So I wanted to put all my usual answers & advice in one place.

I hope this helps someone: 

1. Stop looking for a silver bullet

There is no 1 thing that will explode your newsletter.

You have to test and be consistent with 25 small things that compound.

For example, I send a teaser on X/LI before the newsletter goes out and a snippet after I publish. 


2. Find a recommendation pod

When someone subscribes to your newsletter, you can recommend 4 others.

Team up with 4 operators in your space who recommend you back so every new sub they get sees your newsletter.

Finding the right newsletters to recommend is a huge ROI on your time.


3. Do the math on paid acquisition

It's just two numbers: what you pay per sub, and what you can predictably earn back from them.

If you know what a sub is worth to you (via sponsor revenue, product sales, whatever), you can justify paying $x per email on ads.

For example: drop $5k on fb ads, pull in 2,500 new subs, that's now an extra ~$500 per sponsor slot on every send going forward.


4. Make a lead magnet

Give away something valuable enough that people will hand over their email to get it. 

The trick: pick something that self-selects for your ideal reader. So if they want the magnet, they'll want the newsletter.

I use my actual $2.5M seed pitch deck and $33M series B deck. The only people who want these are founders, which is exactly who I write for.


5. Stop using AI to write

Newsletters live or die on quality. The better your content is, the more you'll grow. 

Every AI pilled person is trying to innovate using complex AI workflows and it’s the wrong way to go.

The whole point is that someone wants your take. I follow Stratechery because I want Ben Thompson’s opinion. 

Your personality is the only hedge against AI.


6. Find a consistent cadence

People always ask “how often should I post?” and it’s the wrong question to ask.

I’ve seen everything work: daily, 3x a week, weekly, monthly. 

The right question is “how do I provide the most value and stay consistent every single week?”

Last thing: this all compounds

Every sub you get today listens to your stories, uses your lead magnets, sends to friends. And the longer you show up, the more people feel like they've been on the journey with you.

It isn’t sexy, but it works.

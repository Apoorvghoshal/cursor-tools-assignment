People used to read blogs because they were worth reading. Writers wrote about their own experiences in their own voice. And while there are some corners of the internet where this still happens, but it feels rare. Tbh, I don't even know how to find long-form B2B content to read outside of Substack.

SEO mostly killed the blog. For all the talk of “writing for humans and not algorithms,” we kind of 𝘩𝘢𝘥 to write for algorithms as a way to hopefully reach humans. 𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝘄𝗿𝗶𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘄𝗮𝘀𝗻’𝘁 𝗮𝗹𝘄𝗮𝘆𝘀 𝗿𝗲𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆 𝗺𝗲𝗮𝗻𝘁 𝘁𝗼 𝗯𝗲 𝗿𝗲𝗮𝗱, 𝗯𝘂𝘁 𝘁𝗼 𝗯𝗲 𝗱𝗶𝘀𝗰𝗼𝘃𝗲𝗿𝗲𝗱.

But with all that said, I still believe content is worth creating. There's more room than ever to write things people actually want to read (and I believe people will read it if you publish it!!!!).

But whether people read or not, there are still v good reasons to publish:

1️⃣ 𝗜𝘁 𝗸𝗲𝗲𝗽𝘀 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝘀𝘁𝗼𝗿𝘆𝘁𝗲𝗹𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗺𝘂𝘀𝗰𝗹𝗲 𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗼𝗻𝗴. The internal clarity you get from articulating your positioning and messaging is vital. The more you do it, the better you become at understanding what you're actually selling.

2️⃣ 𝗜𝘁 𝘀𝗶𝗴𝗻𝗮𝗹𝘀 𝗹𝗲𝗴𝗶𝘁𝗶𝗺𝗮𝗰𝘆. I've developed strong impressions of companies without reading a single blog post. Case studies with impressive logos. A post announcing fundraising. A catalog of content that shows they've been at this for a while. I think "content theater" matters. (h/t Eric Doty for this idea.)

3️⃣ 𝗜𝘁 𝘀𝘁𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗳𝘂𝗲𝗹𝘀 𝗱𝗶𝘀𝗰𝗼𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘆. It's not as direct as it once was, but you still have some control. And it's still the best option you have.

I miss reading blogs. And I miss the days when people shared ideas and experiments in long form. I’m tired of feeds. 😭
